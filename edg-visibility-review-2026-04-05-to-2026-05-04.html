What "average position" actually means

When someone searches a phrase like "dentist Glendale", Google ranks your website somewhere on the results page. Position 1 is the very top result. Positions 1–3 get the most clicks. Positions 1–10 are page one. Anything above 20 means you're on page three or later — most people never scroll that far.

"Not enough data" means your site didn't show up often enough during this period for Google to report a meaningful number — usually a sign that you're either ranked very low or not indexed for that phrase at all.

What we'd recommend next

Three priorities, ordered by expected impact.

  1. Address the visibility gap at Empower Dental Rancho. Zero ranking data across all keywords is a meaningful signal. The next step is a technical audit — confirming the location pages are indexed, internally linked from the main site, and that the local Google Business Profile is connected and verified. We'd expect movement within 4–8 weeks of remediation.
  2. Push the "almost-there" keywords onto page one. Several keywords sit between position 10 and 20 — these are the cheapest wins. Targeted content updates and improved local signals on dentist Glendale, dentist Upland, emergency dentist Glendale, emergency dentist Upland, and dentist Burbank can move them into the top 10, where click-through rates increase 3–5x.
  3. Build out BrightCraft's laser-dentistry positioning. Laser dentistry is a clear differentiator, but Google currently has no signal that BrightCraft offers it. Dedicated service pages with patient-friendly explanations of laser dentistry, laser periodontal therapy, and treatment outcomes would establish topical authority for searches that currently return zero data.
