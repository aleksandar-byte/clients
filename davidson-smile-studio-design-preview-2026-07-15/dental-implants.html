What dental implants do

A stable foundation for replacement teeth.

A dental implant is placed in the jaw to support a replacement tooth or teeth. Unlike a removable denture, the implant itself stays in place. The visible restoration is then designed to restore the missing part of your smile.

Implants may help restore chewing, speaking, and appearance while supporting the bone around the missing-tooth area. They still require daily cleaning and regular dental visits.

Which option fits?

The number of missing teeth changes the plan.

Your exam determines which choices are appropriate. These are the main paths you may discuss at a consultation.

01

One missing tooth

An individual implant can support a single replacement tooth without relying on the neighboring teeth for support.

Often considered when:
  • One tooth is missing
  • The surrounding area is healthy enough for treatment
  • A fixed replacement is preferred
02

Several missing teeth

Implants may support a bridge or another restoration designed to replace multiple teeth with fewer implant posts than teeth.

Often considered when:
  • Several teeth are missing together
  • Stability is an important goal
  • You want to compare fixed and removable paths
03

A full arch of teeth

All-on-4 uses four implants to support a fixed, non-removable full-arch set of teeth. Full dentures remain a removable alternative.

Often considered when:
  • Most or all teeth in an arch are missing
  • Existing teeth have a poor outlook
  • You are comparing fixed and removable full-arch care

What your dentist checks

Implant planning is about more than the gap.

Your dentist will look at the complete picture before recommending treatment. An implant may not be the right first step for every patient.

  • Teeth and gumsActive dental or gum concerns may need attention before implant treatment.
  • Available boneImaging helps assess the area that would support an implant.
  • Health and healingYour health history, medications, and healing factors are part of planning.
  • Your prioritiesComfort, appearance, function, timing, and budget all shape the conversation.

What to expect

From first conversation to your final restoration.

The exact sequence and timing depend on your oral health and the treatment selected.

  1. 01

    Consultation and planning

    Discuss what you want to change, complete an examination, and take appropriate X-rays or 3D imaging to plan the implant position.

  2. 02

    Prepare the area

    Any necessary dental care is addressed first. Some patients may need additional preparation before an implant can be placed.

  3. 03

    Place the implant

    The implant is positioned in the jaw according to the treatment plan. For All-on-4, four implants support a temporary full-arch restoration when clinically appropriate.

  4. 04

    Allow time for healing

    Over the following months, the implant integrates with the surrounding bone. Your dentist will monitor healing and explain care for the area.

  5. 05

    Complete the restoration

    After the implant is ready, a custom final tooth, bridge, or full-arch restoration is attached and checked for fit and function.

Dental implant questions

Helpful answers before your consultation.

How do I know whether I can have dental implants?

An exam and appropriate imaging are needed to assess your teeth, gums, available bone, health history, and goals. Your dentist can then explain whether implants or another option fits your situation.

Can one implant replace more than one tooth?

Depending on the location and treatment plan, implants can support a bridge that replaces several teeth. A full-arch restoration can also be supported by multiple implants.

What is All-on-4?

All-on-4 is a full-arch restoration supported by four implants. A temporary set of teeth may be attached after implant placement when appropriate, followed by a final custom restoration after healing.

How long does implant treatment take?

The sequence varies because the implant needs time to integrate with the jawbone, and some patients need preparatory care. Your personalized plan will explain the expected stages and timing.

Are implants the only option for missing teeth?

No. Partial and full dentures are removable options, and other restorations may be appropriate in some situations. Your dentist can compare the benefits and limitations of the suitable choices.

How do I care for dental implants?

Implants and their restorations need daily brushing, cleaning around and between the teeth, and regular dental visits. Your team will show you how to care for your specific restoration.
