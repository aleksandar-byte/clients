Purpose

1. Purpose and scope

The purpose of this SOP is to standardize Google Business Profile optimization using recurring themes from our local SEO workflow: category accuracy, clean business naming, natural keyword relevance, review velocity, real photos, GBP-to-website alignment, and engagement tracking.

Use this SOP for

  • New GBP setup and profile cleanup.
  • Monthly GBP optimization for local SEO clients.
  • GBP audits for ranking, conversion, or engagement issues.
  • Standardizing review, photo, post, and Q&A workflows.

Do not use it for

  • Fake locations, fake reviews, fake offices, or profile manipulation.
  • Keyword-stuffing names without real-world documentation.
  • Copying competitors blindly without checking business fit.
  • Replacing account-specific judgment or platform compliance checks.
Important: Some local SEO tactics are risky or gray-hat. This SOP keeps the practical parts and flags risky areas clearly.

Workflow

3. Full SOP workflow

Collect account and business data

  • Business name, legal/trade/DBA name, phone, website, address, service areas, hours, categories, services, photos, review history, and competitor list.
  • Collect real-world proof where possible: signage, website, invoices, licenses, storefront photos, branded vehicles, business cards, and directory listings.

Benchmark local competitors

  • Search the main money keywords in the target city/area.
  • Record top 3–10 GBP competitors: name, primary category, secondary categories where detectable, reviews, rating, photos, linked page, and visible services.
  • Do not copy spam. Use competitor data to identify patterns and gaps.

Fix core identity fields first

  • Business name, address/service area, phone, website, hours, and verification status.
  • Do not make multiple major edits at once unless necessary; large identity changes can trigger review or reverification.

Optimize relevance fields

  • Primary category, secondary categories, services, products/menu, description, attributes, and Q&A.
  • Use natural service and city language where it fits.

Improve trust and engagement assets

  • Build review request workflow, reply to reviews, add real photos, publish useful posts, and answer Q&A.
  • Focus on real customer interaction, not artificial activity.

Align the website and citations

  • Link GBP to the best page for the location/service intent.
  • Make sure NAP, services, city coverage, schema, local proof, and internal links support the GBP.

Track, report, and iterate

  • Track calls, clicks, direction requests, bookings, messages, reviews, photo views, map-grid ranking, and competitor movement.
  • Annotate every major change so movement can be tied back to actions.
Field procedure

4. Business name SOP

Objective: Use a clean, real-world business name while avoiding risky keyword stuffing.

Procedure

  1. Confirm the real business name from signage, website header/footer, invoices, business registration, and branded materials.
  2. Compare GBP name against real-world proof.
  3. Remove unsupported keywords, locations, phone numbers, taglines, slogans, emojis, and service lists.
  4. If the client wants a keyword in the name, confirm whether it is part of the real DBA/trade name before changing GBP.
  5. After any name edit, monitor for suspension, reverification, or competitor reporting.

Examples

StatusName
UseAtlas Dental Care
AvoidAtlas Dental Care — Best Dentist Skopje Implants Emergency
Only if documentedAtlas Emergency Dental Care
Risk note: Keyword-stuffed names may appear to rank, but they can trigger profile suspensions, reversions, competitor reports, and reverification. Treat name stuffing as a high-risk tactic.

5. Business description SOP

Objective: Write a clear, conversion-focused description using natural service and city relevance.

Procedure

  1. Identify the primary category, main service, city/area, customer type, and differentiator.
  2. Write the first sentence as: business type + main service + location.
  3. Use the middle section for core services and customer outcomes.
  4. Use the final sentence for trust, USP, or soft CTA.
  5. Remove keyword repetition, sales hype, URLs, all-caps text, and irrelevant claims.

Writing rules

  • Use keywords naturally; do not stuff.
  • Prioritize clarity for the customer.
  • Support every claim somewhere else on GBP or the website.
  • Use services and locations the business actually serves.

Description template

[Business Name] is a [primary category/business type] in [city/area] helping [customer type] with [service 1], [service 2], and [service 3]. We focus on [differentiator: speed, quality, licensed team, experience, specialist work]. Serving [main service area], we help customers [specific outcome or benefit]. Contact us to [simple CTA].

Example

Miller Dental Care is a family dental clinic in Skopje helping adults and children with preventive care, cosmetic dentistry, emergency appointments, and dental implants. Our team focuses on clear treatment plans, modern equipment, and comfortable visits. Contact us to book an appointment.

6. Primary and secondary category SOP

Objective: Choose the most accurate category setup to improve GBP relevance without diluting focus.

Primary category procedure

  1. Search the main service keyword in the target city.
  2. Record the top local pack/map competitors and their visible categories.
  3. Choose the most specific category that describes what the business is, not everything it sells.
  4. Check if the primary category matches the linked website page and GBP services.
  5. Monitor ranking and engagement after category changes.

Secondary category procedure

  1. Add only categories that genuinely describe meaningful parts of the business.
  2. Prioritize categories supported by real services, reviews, photos, and website content.
  3. Avoid unrelated categories just because competitors use them.
  4. Review every 3 months because category availability and competitor behavior change.
Team standard: Primary category is usually the first field to audit when rankings are weak or the GBP is not matching relevant searches.

7. Services, products, and menu SOP

Objective: List real offerings in a way that reinforces GBP relevance and improves customer understanding.

Area How to optimize Avoid
Services Add all real core services. Use common customer search terms. Group closely related services logically. Fake services, duplicate variations, city-stuffed service names, and unsupported services.
Products Use for real products or service packages where it helps conversion. Add photo, name, short description, and price/range where useful. Using products as a messy duplicate of every service without customer benefit.
Menu For food, drink, and service menus, keep items complete, current, and representative. Only listing popular items, outdated pricing, or incomplete menus.

8. Reviews and review replies SOP

Objective: Build a steady, genuine review profile and use replies to support trust, conversion, and natural service relevance.

Review request procedure

  1. Ask every eligible customer after service completion or purchase.
  2. Use one consistent review link or QR code.
  3. Ask for honest, specific feedback; do not script the review.
  4. Avoid unnatural bursts. Build steady velocity over time.
  5. Track reviews gained per month against top competitors.

Reply procedure

  1. Reply to every review within 24–72 hours where possible.
  2. Use a unique response, not a copied template every time.
  3. Mention the service/location only when it sounds natural.
  4. For negative reviews, stay factual and move resolution offline.

Review reply examples

ScenarioReply template
Positive service review Thank you, [Name]. We’re glad we could help with your [service] in [city/area]. We appreciate you choosing [Business Name].
Short positive review Thank you for the review, [Name]. We appreciate your support.
Negative review Thank you for the feedback, [Name]. We’re sorry your experience did not meet expectations. Please contact us at [phone/email] so we can review the details and help resolve this.
Review warning: Do not fake reviews, buy reviews, review-gate, or coach customers to insert exact-match keywords. The safer approach is consistent requests for honest, specific feedback.

9. Photos and media SOP

Objective: Use real images to prove the business is legitimate, improve customer trust, and increase profile engagement.

Must-have photos

  • Exterior/storefront.
  • Interior/customer area.
  • Team/staff.
  • Products/services.
  • Vehicles/equipment if relevant.

Service business photos

  • Before/after work.
  • Job-site photos.
  • Tools and equipment.
  • Uniforms/vehicles.
  • Completed projects.

Upload cadence

  • New setup: 15–30 strong photos.
  • Ongoing: weekly or monthly additions.
  • Replace outdated/low-quality images.
  • Use real photos, not stock.

10. GBP posts SOP

Objective: Use posts for customer communication, conversion, and engagement. Do not treat posts as a guaranteed direct ranking lever.

Post typeUse caseRecommended content
Update General announcement, new service, project highlight. Short headline, real image, 1–2 benefit sentences, CTA.
Offer Discounts, promotions, seasonal offers. Offer details, dates, terms, CTA to call/book.
Event Open days, local events, webinars, special schedules. Event name, date, location, reason to attend.
FAQ-style post Common customer objections or questions. Question headline, concise answer, link or call CTA.
Post template: [Headline with service/offer] [1 sentence explaining the benefit or update] [1 sentence with proof, detail, or urgency] CTA: Call / Book / Learn more / Get directions
Team standard: GBP posts should be used for conversion, freshness, and engagement when the content is genuinely helpful.
Field procedure

11. Q&A SOP

Objective: Use Q&A to answer buying questions, reinforce relevance, and reduce friction before calls or bookings.

Questions to add/answer

  • Do you serve [city/area]?
  • Do you offer [core service]?
  • How fast can I get an appointment?
  • Do you provide emergency service?
  • Where are you located / where do you park?
  • Do you provide warranties or guarantees?
  • What payment methods do you accept?

Answer rules

  • Keep answers short and factual.
  • Use natural service/location terms only where helpful.
  • Do not over-optimize every answer.
  • Mirror important answers on the website FAQ page.
Q: Do you offer [service] in [city]? A: Yes. [Business Name] provides [service] for customers in [city/area]. Contact us to check availability or book an appointment.

12. Address, service area, and proximity SOP

Objective: Set location fields accurately while recognizing that proximity remains a major local ranking factor.

Physical location

  1. Use a real staffed location where customers can visit.
  2. Verify the map pin is accurate.
  3. Add parking/access details through attributes, Q&A, posts, and website content where useful.
  4. Use exterior photos to help customers identify the location.

Service-area business

  1. Use real service areas only.
  2. Do not create fake offices for proximity.
  3. Support service areas with website pages, reviews, photos, and local proof.
  4. Track rankings by grid because performance will vary by searcher location.
Risk note: Virtual addresses, fake offices, and location manipulation are high-risk tactics and should not be used in this SOP.
Field procedure

13. Website and local landing page SOP

Objective: Make the GBP website link reinforce the same business type, services, and location signals as the profile.

Which URL to link

  • One-location business: homepage if it is highly local and service-focused.
  • Multi-location business: specific location page.
  • Multi-service business: best matching service/location page when appropriate.
  • Use UTM tracking on GBP links for reporting.

Landing page requirements

  • NAP matches GBP.
  • Primary service and city are clear in title/H1/body.
  • Core services match GBP services.
  • Local proof: reviews, photos, projects, team, licenses, service areas.
  • Clear CTA above the fold.
  • Mobile-friendly, fast-loading page.
Team standard: Many GBP ranking problems are not GBP-only problems. Weak or misaligned website pages can prevent the profile from performing well.
