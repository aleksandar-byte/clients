Article Brief
Generated Prompt

1. Use the brief as the source of truth.

The article should reflect the provided facts, services, location, audience, and constraints.

Negative
Inventing a specialist, service, offer, or clinic detail that is not in the brief.
Positive
Explaining only the treatments, symptoms, audience, and next steps supported by the brief.

2. Start with a practical answer.

Open with useful guidance before moving into background, causes, mechanisms, and options.

Negative
Starting with generic filler about how important oral health is.
Positive
Answering the patient's likely question in the first paragraph.

3. Write Gutenberg-ready HTML.

Use clean HTML body content only, with no h1 and no Markdown syntax.

Negative
# Heading
**Bold Markdown**
<h1>Article Title</h1>
Positive
<h2>What Patients Should Know</h2>
<p>...</p>

4. Explain without overstepping.

Cover causes, mechanism, evaluation, treatment options, and next steps when relevant, without personalized medical instructions.

Negative
Providing medication dosing, antibiotic guidance, or custom treatment directions.
Positive
Describing why symptoms happen and when a dental evaluation is appropriate.

5. Return only the required JSON.

The final response should be machine-readable JSON with title, metaDescription, sections, and markdown fields.

Negative
Adding notes, explanation, or text before or after the JSON object.
Positive
Returning one valid JSON object where markdown contains the final HTML article body.

6. Keep the tone patient-friendly.

Use plain language, natural phrasing, and a trustworthy professional voice.

Negative
Hype, repetition, obvious AI phrasing, or vague claims.
Positive
Clear explanations that help a patient understand the issue and the practical next step.
Rule: the article body belongs in the markdown JSON field, but it must contain final HTML, not Markdown.
